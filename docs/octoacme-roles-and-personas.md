# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Project Sponsors

### Role Summary
Project Sponsors provide strategic direction, resources, and executive-level support for projects. They champion the project at the organizational level and ensure alignment with business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Define and approve project objectives and key deliverables
- Provide necessary resources and budget allocation
- Remove organizational impediments and barriers
- Make high-level decisions on scope and priorities
- Communicate project value to executive leadership

### Goals
- Ensure projects align with strategic business objectives
- Maximize return on investment
- Maintain stakeholder confidence and support

### Typical Communication
- Monthly executive briefings and steering committee meetings
- Strategic decision points and approval gates
- High-level status updates and escalation resolution

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They capture and document requirements, ensuring that stakeholder needs are translated into actionable plans for development teams.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Translate business needs into functional specifications
- Facilitate communication between stakeholders and technical teams
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business requirements

### Goals
- Ensure clear, actionable requirements
- Minimize rework due to requirement misunderstandings
- Maximize alignment between business needs and technical solutions

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- User stories and acceptance criteria documentation
- Regular collaboration with Product Managers and Developers
- Coordination with QA Lead on testing requirements

---

## QA Leads

### Role Summary
QA Leads oversee quality assurance processes and standards across the project. They create comprehensive test strategies, coordinate testing activities, and ensure that deliverables meet quality expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality standards and testing strategies
- Create and maintain test plans and test cases
- Coordinate user acceptance testing (UAT) activities
- Track and report on quality metrics and defects
- Mentor team members on testing best practices

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality, defect-free deliverables
- Catch issues early in the development cycle
- Maintain comprehensive test coverage

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews and quality gate checkpoints
- Daily standup participation and bug triage meetings
- Close collaboration with Developers and Business Analysts
- UAT coordination with stakeholders

---

## Change Managers

### Role Summary
Change Managers facilitate the adoption of new processes, tools, and systems. They ensure stakeholders are prepared for changes and manage communication throughout transitions.

### Responsibilities
- Develop change management and communication plans
- Assess stakeholder readiness and resistance
- Create training materials and conduct training sessions
- Monitor adoption and address change-related issues
- Coordinate rollout activities with deployment teams

### Goals
- Maximize user adoption and minimize resistance
- Ensure smooth transitions with minimal disruption
- Build organizational capability for future changes

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder impact assessments and readiness reports
- Training sessions and user communications
- Collaboration with Project Sponsors on change strategy
- Coordination with Deployment Coordinators on rollout timing

---

## Deployment Coordinators

### Role Summary
Deployment Coordinators schedule, execute, and monitor software releases and deployments. They ensure smooth transitions to production and coordinate response to post-deployment issues.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule release windows
- Coordinate deployment activities across teams
- Execute deployment checklists and runbooks
- Monitor post-release metrics and issues
- Communicate deployment status to stakeholders

### Goals
- Achieve zero-downtime deployments
- Minimize post-deployment incidents
- Maintain clear communication during releases

### Typical Communication
- Deployment schedules and readiness assessments
- Go/no-go decision meetings
- Real-time deployment status updates
- Post-deployment reports and retrospectives

---

## Role Interactions and Collaboration

### Business Analyst Collaboration
- Works closely with **Project Managers** to finalize clear requirements and project scope
- Primarily collaborates with **Developers** to ensure technical feasibility
- Partners with **QA Lead** to define acceptance criteria and testing requirements
- Coordinates with **Product Managers** to align business requirements with product vision

### Change Manager Collaboration
- Collaborates with **Project Sponsors** to develop change strategies and secure executive support
- Partners with **Deployment Coordinators** to align communication with deployment schedules
- Works with **Project Managers** to integrate change activities into project plans
- Coordinates with end-users and stakeholders regarding major changes or launches

### QA Lead Collaboration
- Works closely with **Developers** throughout the development cycle
- Collaborates with **Business Analysts** to understand requirements and define test cases
- Coordinates with **Deployment Coordinators** to ensure proper testing before releases
- Reports quality metrics to **Project Managers** and **Product Managers**

### Deployment Coordinator Collaboration
- Partners with **Developers** to execute technical deployment steps
- Coordinates with **Change Managers** for user communication and training timing
- Works with **Project Managers** to align releases with project milestones
- Collaborates with **QA Lead** to ensure release readiness

### Project Sponsor Collaboration
- Provides strategic direction to **Project Managers** and **Product Managers**
- Supports **Change Managers** in driving organizational adoption
- Makes key decisions in partnership with all project leadership roles
- Champions project success at the executive level
